How to Draw Cylinders and Ellipses

Ovals vs ellipses

A lot of people seem to be confused about the difference between the two, so let's get that out of the way..

All ellipses are ovals. But not all ovals are ellipses. An egg-like oval is not an ellipse. Ellipses must have 4 equal quadrants.

To fit an ellipse inside the square plane, look for a few key clues:

  1. A circle inside a square always touches the square at the middle of every edge, creating four points of contact. To find the exact midpoints, I use the X method. The intersection of the X helps us find the centers of all the edges.
  2. The minor axis is at the same angle as the receding lines of the box and crosses the center point.
  3. The major axis is perpendicular to the minor axis. It doesn't pass through the exact center of the square; it's slightly farther away due to foreshortening.
