Philosophical Reflections and Ethical Considerations

Morality, Ethics, and Agency

  • The purpose of morality and ethics is discussed, suggesting that personal preferences and desires ultimately guide moral and ethical decisions. Preferences such as happiness, smiling children, puppies, and good food are highlighted as personal choices, not inherently given by entropy or divine entities.
  • It's crucial to distinguish between epistemological truths, moral values, and aesthetic preferences. People often conflate these concepts, leading to ethical confusion. Understanding the differences is essential to avoid conflating technical concepts like entropy with moral or ideological positions.
  • Epistemological "oughts" can be derived from reality, guiding actions based on desired outcomes or knowledge acquisition. Decision-theoretic "oughts" suggest actions based on the goal of winning or achieving a specific end. Aesthetic values, however, are subjective preferences that cannot be derived from objective reality.
  • The absence of divine judgment in moral matters implies a terrifying freedom where personal values may irreconcilably differ. There's no universal metric for comparing values, making it challenging to argue for the superiority of one set of values over another.

  • High coherence enables safer exploration of dangerous spaces by ensuring self-control and preventing accidental harm. Coherence in search strategies allows for navigating complex spaces safely, akin to cells having rigid cell walls to protect their internal environment. Life itself is an example of order amidst entropy, suggesting that coherence is fundamental to life.
