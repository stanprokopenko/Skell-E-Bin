How to Draw Torsos as Boxes

Copy Code

When drawing the human figure, it's helpful to simplify the torso into a box-like shape. Imagine the body with the limbs, arms, legs, and neck removed, focusing on the area from the shoulders down to the hips. This simplified form creates a wide, tall box that you can manipulate in various ways.

The goal is to turn, twist, and lean this box to create dynamic poses. You can also bloat certain areas to represent the rib cage or hips. This exercise helps you understand the basic structure of the torso without getting distracted by anatomical details.

Drawing Torso Boxes from Imagination (Level 1)

Start by drawing these simplified torso boxes from your imagination. This approach allows you to focus on the basic form without the distraction of reference images.

To begin:

  1. Visualize the shoulder plane as the top of your box.
  2. Draw the front, back, and sides of the box coming down from the shoulders.
  3. Imagine how the hips will move in relation to the shoulders.
  4. Add a center line to help visualize the form.
  5. Divide the box into sections with cross-contour lines.

Experiment with different proportions. A shorter, wider box might represent a husky build, while a narrower, longer box could indicate a tall, skinny person.

Techniques for Manipulating Box Forms

To create dynamic poses, use these techniques to manipulate your box forms:

  • Twisting: Rotate the top of the box in relation to the bottom.
  • Curving: Bend the box along its length.
  • Bloating: Expand certain areas, like the rib cage, to add volume.
  • Tapering: Narrow the box towards one end.

When twisting the box, pay attention to how the corners and planes shift. The center line will help you visualize this movement.

Drawing Torso Boxes from Photos (Level 2)

Drawing from photos can be more challenging because the visual information can be distracting. Focus on interpreting the overall shape and gesture rather than copying anatomical details.

When working from a photo:

  1. Identify the main angles of the shoulders and hips.
  2. Visualize the box form underneath the visible anatomy.
  3. Sketch the main planes of the box.
  4. Add the center line to show the twist or curve of the torso.
  5. Refine the shape, adding subtle curves or bloating where necessary.

Remember, you're interpreting the photo, not copying it exactly. The goal is to capture the essence of the pose in a simplified form.

Importance of Simplification and Interpretation

Simplifying the torso into a box form is more challenging than it might seem. Avoid the temptation to draw detailed anatomy or copy the exact shapes you see in a photo. The goal is to interpret and simplify.

Focus on:

  • The overall gesture and movement of the torso
  • The main angles of the shoulders and hips
  • The twist or curve of the spine
  • The basic proportions of the torso

This simplified approach will help you understand the underlying structure of the figure, which is crucial for more advanced figure drawing.

Exaggeration Techniques

Exaggeration can make your poses more dynamic and interesting. To exaggerate effectively:

  1. Identify the baseline or neutral pose.
  2. Observe how the actual pose differs from the neutral position.
  3. Push those differences further, but subtly.

For example, if the shoulders are leaning slightly forward, exaggerate by tilting them more dramatically. If there's a slight twist, increase the rotation.

Be careful not to overdo it. Subtle exaggerations often work best, maintaining believability while enhancing the dynamism of the pose.

Practice Guidelines

To improve your torso box drawing skills:

  • Fill multiple pages with these simplified forms.
  • Aim for at least 20 different torso boxes.
  • Experiment with various angles, twists, and poses.
  • If you struggle with certain poses, try them again with slight variations.
  • Post challenging drawings for critique and feedback.

Remember, the goal is to become comfortable with visualizing and manipulating these simplified forms. With practice, you'll develop a stronger understanding of the torso's structure and movement, which will greatly enhance your figure drawing skills.
