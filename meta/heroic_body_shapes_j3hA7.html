Heroic Body Shapes

Copy Code

When I start drawing different characters, I always consider their body shapes and costumes first. It's crucial to understand that in the early stages, the core or center mass of the character remains the same, and I adjust its size to fit the specific character I'm envisioning. This concept of a consistent core in character design is fundamental to creating diverse and unique characters.

Adjusting the Core for Character Diversity

To achieve character diversity, I make various adjustments to the core's size and proportions. These adjustments can include making the chest bigger, smaller, or altering the character's height into different shapes. Initially, I think in terms of a standard human form.

  • For a shorter, stocky character, I maintain the same core but reposition the head and focus on the character's volume and the space they occupy.
  • After establishing the human core, I determine the general areas where limbs and other body parts will be, without focusing on details.
  • For a long, skinny character, I keep the core the same but adjust the pelvic area and limb lengths accordingly.
  • When drawing female characters, I apply the same principles but might emphasize the hips more.

It's important to note that the core remains consistent across all characters, regardless of size or position, including characters in lying positions.

Proportions and Comic Book Characters

In comic book art, making the character's head slightly smaller than anatomically correct proportions can make them appear more heroic. The standard for comic book characters is often to aim for a height of nine heads tall, adjusting the head size to achieve a slightly smaller than normal appearance. This approach to character design emphasizes the importance of maintaining a consistent core while allowing for variations to define unique character traits.

* * *

The key to drawing different characters lies in the balance between maintaining a consistent core and allowing for variations that define unique character traits. By adjusting the core for character diversity and considering specific proportions for comic book characters, artists can create a wide range of characters that are both unique and recognizable.
