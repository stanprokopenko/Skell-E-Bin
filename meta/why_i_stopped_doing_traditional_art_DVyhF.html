Why I Stopped Doing Traditional Art

Copy Code

I've come to realize the importance of narrowing down my focus in art. This decision came from understanding that trying to master everything was not only impractical but also misleading. Observing highly skilled artists often doesn't show the thousands of hours of practice and the journey behind their mastery, which can give a skewed perception of what it takes to achieve such levels of skill.

The Challenge of Mastery

The journey of learning art is filled with complexity and vastness, making it overwhelming to try and become proficient in all its forms. As I've grown older, the need to specialize in certain areas has become more apparent, especially if I want to excel in what I truly wish to achieve.

Balancing Art and Life

Balancing art practice with daily life responsibilities, such as family time, taxes, personal care, and exercise, is a significant challenge. I've learned that dedicating all my time to drawing and painting could lead to burnout and a loss of enjoyment in both life and art. This balance is crucial for sustaining a healthy relationship with my craft and personal well-being.

Focusing on Career Goals

To succeed on platforms like YouTube and in my career as an artist, focusing on specific skills while letting go of others has been essential. Although I enjoy figure drawing, it doesn't align with my long-term goals or contribute to my desired career path. Choosing activities that I can see myself doing for decades is crucial for a sustainable creative career.

The Role of Teaching and External Motivations

Teaching art has provided me with motivation and a deeper understanding of the craft. It involves explaining concepts and engaging with students, which in turn, enriches my own practice. External motivations, such as participating in gallery shows or doing commissions, have also helped sustain my interest in practices like figure drawing.

Reevaluating Traditional Art

While traditional art holds a special place in my heart, it doesn't fit into my long-term goals and career plans. This realization has led me to set clear goals for the future, helping me decide how to allocate my time and which skills to focus on, even if it means making some short-term sacrifices.

Setting Clear Goals

Setting clear goals has been a pivotal part of my journey. It has allowed me to focus on what truly matters and make decisions that align with my future aspirations. Personal interests and goals evolve over time, and it's possible to return to previously set-aside practices when they align with current objectives.

Embracing Limitations

Embracing my limitations and focusing on specific projects has enhanced my personal fulfillment and made my work more meaningful. Drawing parallels from personal experiences, like enjoying long drives during a cross-country trip, has highlighted the value of context and purpose in making activities enjoyable. Limiting options and focusing on specific art forms or projects has helped me overcome indecision and find a personal style.

* * *

This journey has emphasized the importance of defining goals and making focused efforts rather than abandoning certain art forms because they are inherently less valuable. Traditional art is valuable and worth pursuing, but achieving goals in art requires focus and the willingness to make sacrifices.
