Transferring Your Layout

To begin transforming your reduced size layout into a full-size comic page, you'll need to trace it onto a new, larger sheet. This can be done using either a light box or a computer monitor. When doing this, your focus should be on the loose placement of elements rather than replicating every detail precisely.

Establishing Panel Borders and Perspective

Once your layout is transferred, the next step is to establish the panel borders. This involves drawing the outlines of each panel on your page. After the borders are in place, you'll need to lay in the proper perspective for each panel. This is particularly crucial for panels that depict larger scenes, as getting the perspective right is key to a believable space.

Sketching and Tightening Elements

With the panel borders and perspective established, you can start to re-establish your panels. This involves lightly sketching each element within the panels before tightening up these sketches. During this phase, it's important to use proper construction techniques for drawing faces, figures, and environmental elements. At this point, you should aim to create the cleanest, simplest version of your page, avoiding too much detail.

Adding Details and Rendering

After your page has been re-established with tight line drawings, you can begin to add line weight, shadows, and rendering. This step allows you to bring depth and dimension to your drawings. It's important to work systematically through the page, which will help you to identify and fix any issues that arise during this stage.

Digital Work and Posting for Critique

If you prefer working digitally, feel free to use your preferred digital tools and methods throughout this process. Once you've completed your full-size page, you should post images of both your final reduced size layout and your final pencils. By posting your assignments within the next month, you'll have a chance to be featured in a critique video. This is a great opportunity to receive feedback and improve your skills.

Good luck with your assignment, and remember to approach each step methodically to achieve the best results!
