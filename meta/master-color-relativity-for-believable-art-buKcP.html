Optical Illusions with Colors

Consider a shirt that looks green in one picture, gold in another, and red in yet another. Despite these differences, the shirt is actually the same color in all images. This phenomenon occurs because of the surrounding colors and lighting conditions. For instance, in a blue environment, the shirt might look orange, while in a green setting, it appears red. This is due to how our eyes perceive color in relation to other colors around it.

When you look at an image and try to pick out colors, you might think you're seeing greens and blues. However, upon closer inspection, you might find that what you thought was green is actually orange, and what seemed blue is just a shade of gray. This is because the colors are influenced by the surrounding hues, making them appear different than they actually are.

Practical Application in Painting

Let's apply these concepts to a painting study. Take a painting called "Early Morning" by Alexandra Jacob. Start by finding the colors of the sky. You might see a shift from lighter desaturated yellow to oranges. If you think a color is purple, sample it and see that it's actually a desaturated purple. When you paint with this color, it looks very purple compared to the surrounding colors.

To achieve realistic effects, use a limited palette and shift colors slightly around the color wheel. For instance, if you want a color to look purple, desaturate it and shift it towards red. This way, it will appear purple compared to the orange around it. Similarly, for greens, shift towards yellow and desaturate. This technique helps you pick colors more accurately and create believable light situations.

Summary and Importance

Understanding color relativity is crucial for artists. It helps you see how colors interact and influence each other, leading to more accurate and realistic artwork. By observing and understanding color temperature and relativity, you can improve your painting skills and create more convincing light and color effects. So, as you do your color studies, remember to look closely at the colors and how they relate to each other. This knowledge will dramatically enhance your artwork.
