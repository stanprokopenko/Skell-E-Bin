Comic Lighting and Shadow Techniques

Establishing the Basic Composition

We start with a simple design pattern for the panel:

  • Arrange figures in a compositional shape
  • Outline figures with a dark lighting pattern that opens up to draw the eye to the middle
  • Include foreground elements to frame the panel

Adding Line Weights

Begin by shadowing underneath objects to add depth:

  • Start with easy areas, such as underneath figures and objects
  • Press hard for line weights, but be careful not to overdo it
  • Use line weights to clean up details without focusing on the figures themselves

Foreground and Background Line Weights

  • Use thicker lines for foreground elements and thinner lines for background elements
    • Be careful not to inadvertently use thick lines on background elements, as it can flatten the image
  • Finish the foreground first, focusing on the most detailed area

Compositional Shadows

Add compositional shadows to frame the main figures and direct focus:

  • Shadow out elements on one side to create a localized light and frame the main figures
  • Use shadows to create turning and dimension on objects
  • Consider using X's to indicate areas to be darkened, but be mindful of visualizing the final result

Add shadows behind main figures to push them up from the wall compositionally.

Layering Darks and Lights

Create depth and dimension by layering darks and lights throughout the panel:

  • Alternate between dark and light areas to push elements back and create depth

Refining Details and Shadows

  • Clean up shadows and make them more crisp
  • Add texture and detail to main figures, being mindful of the small working space
  • Be deliberate and precise with shadows and line weights, while maintaining a relatively basic compositional framework
  • Rely on the inker to understand the penciler's intentions, as perfectly clean pencils are difficult to achieve on a schedule

By following these steps and being mindful of the composition, line weights, and shadows, we can create a comic book panel with great depth and dimension that effectively guides the viewer's eye to the main focus of the scene.
