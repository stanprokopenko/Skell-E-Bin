General Advice on Sketching

Importance of Practice for Improvement

Keep practicing, and you'll get better at it. The more you practice, the more you'll be able to visualize the forms and get those lines to wrap around correctly.

Visualization of Forms and Wrapping Lines Correctly

The body of the lobster looks pretty good. The cross contours on the tail are wrapping around nicely. Just keep working on it.

Use of Cross Contours for Better Results

Use little lines representing cross contours on the inside for better results. This helps in making random blobs feel like 3D forms with consistent cross-contours.

Suggestion to Use Red or Blue Pencils for Sketching Big Masses

Try using a red or blue pencil to lightly sketch big masses and adjust slowly. This can help in visualizing the forms better.

Reference to Dynamic Sketching Lessons

Refer to dynamic sketching lessons for practicing different versions of sketching lines. This can help in thinking of large simple forms translating into larger three-dimensional forms.

Tips for Improving Form Visualization and Line Quality

Thinking of Large Simple Forms

Think of large simple forms translating into larger three-dimensional forms. Visualize simple primary forms before secondary and tertiary forms.

Making Random Blobs Feel Like 3D Forms

Make random blobs feel like 3D forms with consistent cross-contours.

Adding Additional Axes for More Active Lines

Add additional axes for more active lines in characters.

Treating Overlapping Contours as Separate Parts

Treat overlapping contours as separate parts to enhance depth.

Showing Roundness of Edges

Show the roundness of edges for a more bloated pancake effect.
