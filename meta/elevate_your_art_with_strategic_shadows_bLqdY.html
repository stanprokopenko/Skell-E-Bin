Initial Composition and Setup

I started with a very simple design pattern for the panel, positioning figures together in a compositional shape. I then outlined them with a dark lighting pattern to draw the viewer's eye into the middle, using elements in the foreground to frame the panel. This setup established all elements without shadow, line weights, or rendering.

Starting with Shadows and Line Weights

Knowing the lighting was coming from above, I began by shadowing underneath objects and adding line weights. This approach allowed me to add depth without making complex compositional decisions about lighting. I focused on making areas under objects darker, gradually establishing line weights to enhance the sense of depth. For figures, areas like under a leg were darkened, and I pressed hard to emphasize these shadows, though I noted this might not be advisable for everyone.

Enhancing Foreground and Background

I moved on to the foreground, making lines thicker for closer objects to emphasize their proximity. This technique helps to create a sense of depth, with thicker lines for closer objects and thinner lines for those in the background, preventing the image from flattening. I carefully outlined the foreground to make it stand out, using shadows to add dimension to elements like debris by drawing shadows rather than the objects themselves.

Compositional Lighting and Detailing

I then focused on compositional lighting, aiming to highlight the main figures and elements. I avoided using X's for dark areas, preferring to draw shadows directly to maintain control over how they interact. I detailed the main figures lightly to avoid overcomplication, considering the small working size. Adding shadows behind characters helped to push them forward compositionally, ensuring they remained the focal point.

Refining Shadows and Adding Final Touches

As I refined the shadows, making them crisper and adding detail through shadowing, I paid attention to separating elements clearly. I used a piece of paper under my hand to prevent smudging, especially as I worked with a very hard lead. I aimed to delineate shapes clearly before shadowing inside them, though I acknowledged that sometimes the overall shape emerges more organically. I added character to elements like pipes through line weight and shadow, ensuring they contributed to guiding the viewer's eye into the image.

* * *

In conclusion, the panel's design was effective in drawing attention through a simple yet strategic use of dark and light patterns. I emphasized the importance of layering darks and lights to add depth and dimension, maintaining a basic compositional framework throughout the process. This approach allowed for a gradual build-up of complexity, ensuring the viewer's eye is drawn to the intended focal points while keeping the overall composition cohesive and engaging.
