In this journey of mastering digital painting, we're diving into a structured approach that spans several weeks, focusing on new tools, mastering techniques, and the concept of thinking in layers. Let's break down this learning path to ensure you get the most out of your digital painting experience.

We're kicking things off with some exciting new tools that are expected to revolutionize your painting process. These include:

  • Layers
  • Marquees
  • A new blender brush

The introduction of these tools is particularly thrilling because of their potential to simplify the blending of colors, a fundamental aspect of digital painting that can often be challenging for artists.

Mastering the Tools in the Initial Weeks

The first two weeks are all about getting comfortable with these new tools. Here's what we'll focus on:

  • Familiarizing ourselves with the brushes is crucial. Each brush has its unique properties and understanding these will allow you to make more informed choices in your painting.
  • We'll also emphasize the importance of practicing painting to grasp the nuances of hard and soft edges. This practice is key to replicating and deeply understanding the subjects you observe.
  • The goal during these initial weeks is to become comfortable with the tools. This comfort is essential for accurately replicating and understanding the subjects you're painting.

Exploring the Concept of Thinking in Layers

Moving beyond the basics, we'll delve into a more conceptual aspect of digital painting:

  • We'll explore the idea of thinking in layers. This doesn't refer to Photoshop layers in the traditional sense, but rather to mental lighting layers. It's about understanding how light interacts with different materials and textures.
  • Our discussions will cover how to differentiate between various types of light, such as highlights, diffuse light, bounce light, and refraction. This knowledge is crucial for enhancing the realism and depth of your artwork.

Encouragement for Continuous Practice

I cannot stress enough the importance of continuous practice and experimentation:

  • While there are three official assignments, I encourage you to keep painting and experimenting beyond these. The more you paint, the quicker you'll learn and the easier the process will become.
  • You'll find additional resources and assignments in the downloads folder to support your learning journey.
  • I'm eager to see your artwork and encourage you to create continuously. Your progress and creations are what make teaching this course so rewarding.

In conclusion, this structured approach to mastering digital painting is designed to build your skills progressively, from understanding new tools to conceptualizing light and materials in your artwork. Remember, the key to mastery is practice, so keep experimenting, keep learning, and most importantly, keep painting.
