20 Ways: How To Make Money As An Artist (Ranked from Best to Worst)

Becoming a professional artist is a common goal among many who wish to pursue their passion full-time while still being able to support themselves financially. However, the art market is saturated, making it challenging to sell work and earn a good income from art. Despite this, it's possible to find success, but it requires a balance between creating art and producing commercial goods. This balance is crucial to maintain artistic integrity and respect within the art world.

Artists need to pursue artistic quality while also actively seeking ways to generate income. Selling artwork is the primary way for artists to make money, and creating a natural demand for one's work is essential. Exhibiting work, especially through art galleries, is the most effective and industry-accepted method for selling art and maintaining artistic integrity. However, artists must navigate the challenges of the art market and find ways to distinguish themselves and their work.

Selling Artwork

Selling artwork is the primary way for artists to make money. It's crucial to focus on creating a natural demand for one's work to facilitate sales. This involves distinguishing between creating art for the sake of art and producing commercial goods. The goal is to maintain artistic integrity while also generating income.

Art galleries play a significant role in selling art and maintaining integrity. Exhibiting work through galleries is the most effective and industry-accepted method. However, it's important to approach galleries correctly. Cold calling galleries is not effective and is generally frowned upon. Instead, artists should look for alternative ways to exhibit their work, such as applying to open calls, renting exhibition spaces, or hosting open studio days.

Galleries that charge artists fees without focusing on making sales, known as vanity art galleries, have drawbacks. They may not be the best option for artists looking to sell their work and maintain their integrity.

In summary, selling artwork through galleries and creating natural demand are key strategies for artists to earn money while preserving their artistic integrity.

Alternative Exhibition Methods

Cold calling galleries and vanity galleries come with significant drawbacks. Cold calling is generally ineffective and frowned upon in the art industry. Vanity galleries, which charge artists fees without a focus on making sales, are not ideal for artists aiming to sell their work and maintain integrity.

On the other hand, artist residencies, art stipends, and grants offer substantial benefits. Artist residencies provide a supportive environment for artists to develop their work, often including studio space, materials, lodging, and sometimes a stipend. Art stipends and grants financially support artists to work on projects, often favoring those with a social or political message.

Art prizes, awards, and contests present opportunities for income and recognition. They can offer cash prizes and exhibition opportunities, enhancing an artist's resume and potentially boosting their career. Being selected in an art contest is a form of validation and beneficial for an artist's resume. However, it's crucial to identify genuine art contests by considering factors like the application fee, jury, contest history, exhibition opportunities, and the reputation of the organizing entity. The best art contests often don't have an application fee and are funded by public or private entities, featuring a renowned jury and a long-standing history.

Public Art Projects and Open Calls

Public art projects and open calls offer unique opportunities for artists to showcase their work and potentially earn income. However, these opportunities often come with the challenge of tailoring art to specific criteria set by the project or call. This can be both an opportunity and a challenge, as it requires artists to adapt their work to meet these criteria while still maintaining their artistic integrity and voice.

Art Fairs

Art fairs can be a lucrative opportunity for artists, even those without gallery representation. However, participating in art fairs may raise concerns about artistic integrity.

Self-represented artists at art fairs might be perceived as less successful, and their work could be seen as more commercial. This perception can impact an artist's reputation and how their work is valued within the art community.

Despite these concerns, art fairs remain a viable option for artists looking to sell their work and potentially increase their income. It's important for artists to weigh the potential financial benefits against the possible impact on their artistic integrity.

Online Marketplaces and Prints

Online marketplaces vary significantly in quality and acceptance within the art industry. Industry-accepted platforms like Artsy require gallery representation, which can be a barrier for many artists. Other marketplaces are oversaturated and may offer less benefit for an artist's career. It's important to research and choose platforms that align with your goals and maintain your artistic integrity.

Selling art prints is another option that is generally accepted within the industry. However, it's considered less lucrative compared to selling original works. Additionally, if not managed carefully, selling prints can potentially devalue the original work. Artists should consider the implications of selling prints and how it fits into their overall strategy for income and career development.

Commissions and Art Books

Accepting commissions offers immediate sales but comes with its own set of challenges. One of the main issues is the limitation on artistic freedom. When working on a commission, artists often have to adhere to the client's requests, which may not always align with their own artistic vision. This can potentially harm an artist's integrity if they feel they are compromising too much on their creative expression.

Selling art books or artist editions is another strategy for artists looking to make money. However, book printing can be expensive and time-consuming. It requires a decent audience or collector's base to sell books and cover the costs involved. Despite these challenges, art books can serve as a statement or a printed portfolio, which is particularly beneficial for photographers. They add credibility to an artist's resume and can be a valuable part of their overall body of work.

Profitability from book sales often requires existing demand and good sales of an artist's work. Therefore, it's important for artists to consider whether they have the necessary audience and demand for their books before investing in this strategy.

Art-Related Side Hustles

Art-related side hustles can provide additional income but may have an impact on an artist's career in the long term. It's important to choose side hustles that align with your career goals and maintain your artistic integrity.

  • Being an art speaker allows you to share your experiences and participate in art discussions. This can enhance your credibility in the art world. However, finding opportunities to speak can be challenging.
  • Working as an art curator is seen as authoritative and can benefit an artist's profile. This role often requires an academic background or experience in the field.
  • Teaching art is a way to combine sharing your skills with making a living. It allows you to teach without compromising your personal techniques, in a setting that is accepted within the art industry.

Choosing the right side hustles can contribute positively to your career, offering both financial benefits and opportunities for professional growth.

Digital Platforms and Merchandising

Patreon and crowdfunding are modern income sources that allow fans to support artists with monthly contributions or fund specific projects. However, Patreon requires a large, engaged audience to be effective, and crowdfunding is not typically lucrative for general artist support.

YouTube offers monetization through ad income, but it can be frowned upon in the art world unless the content maintains artistic integrity. Similarly, blogging about art should be done discreetly and separate from an artist's professional profile due to competition and credibility concerns.

Offering online art courses risks compromising artistic integrity, especially if selling personal techniques. Affiliate marketing through social media can damage an artist's credibility and requires a large following to be profitable.

Merchandising artwork on products is seen as commercializing art and can be harmful to an artist's reputation in the high-end art world. It's important to consider the long-term impact on your career and reputation when exploring these digital platforms and merchandising opportunities.

* * *

For success in the art world, it's crucial to adopt long-term, sustainable strategies. These strategies should focus on maintaining credibility and artistic integrity. One key recommendation is to have a stable source of income outside of art sales. This approach helps avoid compromising artistic integrity due to financial pressures.

Creating natural demand for one's art and focusing on quality work are essential. By prioritizing the creation of compelling and high-quality art, artists can generate interest and demand naturally, leading to better sales opportunities and financial success.

In summary, the path to making good money in the art industry involves a careful balance between artistic expression and strategic income generation. By focusing on quality, seeking out supportive opportunities, and maintaining integrity, artists can navigate the challenges of the art market and achieve long-term success.
