The Creator's Perspective on the Internet and Algorithms

I have mixed feelings about the internet and algorithms. On one hand, I find them fascinating and enjoy the interaction they facilitate between creators and audiences. The thrill of receiving rewards from algorithms, whether positive or negative, is something I find oddly satisfying, even when it leads to personal outrage or failure. Comparing the internet to a slot machine and flying in a wingsuit through treacherous terrain, I relish the challenge and excitement of navigating the complex landscape of online communication. My affection for the internet isn't limited to its positive aspects; I also find the unpredictable nature of algorithm-driven content compelling.

The Challenges of Algorithm-Driven Content

The shift from human curation to reliance on highly intelligent algorithms for content recommendation has its advantages, but it also comes with significant drawbacks, particularly in how it affects our attention and satisfaction. Algorithms are designed to maximize the time users spend on a website, which can diverge significantly from what actually makes users happy or fulfilled. This unpredictable nature of algorithm-driven content is a key challenge.

Launching a Curated Newsletter

In response to these reflections, we're relaunching the Nerd Fighteria newsletter as a more curated, human-driven content recommendation service. Our aim is to highlight the best of the internet regardless of its direct relevance to our usual topics. This decision to offer a human-driven content recommendation service is our way of navigating the challenges posed by algorithm-driven content.

Goals of the Newsletter

The newsletter aims to capture a sense of being part of the flawed yet beautiful experience of humanity. We aim to provide content that deepens readers' understanding of the world and the people in it, without succumbing to the extremes of overly positive or negative news. We encourage sign-up for the newsletter, emphasizing the desire for a new way to communicate and share content beyond the constraints of algorithm-driven platforms.
