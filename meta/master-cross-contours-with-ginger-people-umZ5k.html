In this tutorial, we're going to draw ginger root people. No need for red markers; we'll use a red pencil. This idea builds on the level one project where you turn ginger blobs into characters.

Basic Approach

Start with random ginger blobs. Make them humanoid by adding limbs and a head. Focus on mass rather than perfect anatomy and proportions.

Adding Dynamic Poses

Use photos of people in dynamic poses. Ensure the photos have depth (limbs coming towards you, body twisting). Avoid static poses.

Increasing Difficulty

Create specific characters, like a monster ogre with a club. More specific characters add more constraints and chances for errors. Random humanoid blobs are more forgiving and useful for practicing visualizing form.

Initial Sketching

Start with a torso and head. Focus on the shape and gesture. Add limbs and other body parts as blobs. Keep the sketch light and exploratory.

Refining the Drawing

Switch to a black pencil for cleanup. Focus on line weight and designing shapes. Add cross contours to show depth and volume. Emphasize interesting shapes and avoid symmetry.

Example: Drawing a Ginger Root Person

  1. Start with a torso and head: Begin with a blobby torso and add a head.
  2. Add limbs and other body parts as blobs: Think about the overall shape and gesture.
  3. Use cross contours to show depth: Add subtle lines to indicate the form.
  4. Clean up the drawing with a black pencil: Refine the shapes and add line weight.
  5. Emphasize interesting shapes and avoid symmetry: Make the design dynamic and engaging.

Example: Drawing a Specific Character

Final Touches

Ensure a consistent ground plane for all characters. Add background elements if necessary, like a hill. Make adjustments to improve the overall composition.
