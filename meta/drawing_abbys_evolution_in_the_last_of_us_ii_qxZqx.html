Drawing Abby's Evolution in "The Last of Us II"

Copy Code

In our class, we've journeyed through the steps from gesture to skeleton, to construction to muscle, and light and shadow, aiming to weave these elements seamlessly into our drawings. Today, I'll be using Abby from "The Last of Us Part II" as a case study to demonstrate these concepts, focusing on her character development and the physical transformations she undergoes throughout the story.

We start with an overview of the drawing process, from the initial gesture to the final touches of light and shadow. Abby's character in "The Last of Us Part II" serves as an excellent example for this process, showcasing how to integrate various drawing elements to vividly convey character development.

Abby's Character Evolution

Abby's character undergoes significant evolution, depicted through three distinct acts: youthful, vengeful, and emaciated. This evolution is not just a narrative device but is deeply reflected in her physical and emotional journey.

  • Her powerful, rigid pose conveys stability and strength, marking a departure from traditional feminine archetypes. This suggests a blend of masculine and feminine qualities, sparking discussions about her character design and its departure from overly sexualized female characters.
  • Abby's posture and body language evolve significantly, mirroring her character arc. From a carefree youth to a rigid, vengeful stance, and finally to a weakened, emaciated form, each stage reflects her emotional and physical journey.
  • The transition in Abby's physicality is illustrated through changes in her gesture, weight-bearing leg, and center of gravity, showcasing her emotional and physical transformation.
  • Additionally, Abby's facial features and body structure evolve, moving from a more feminine appearance to a more masculine build, and finally to a state reflecting hardship and change.

Drawing Process and Techniques

Gesture and Skeleton

  • The process begins by identifying the unique qualities of Abby's pose. Understanding the skeleton is crucial for capturing the character's physical form accurately.

Construction and Muscle

  • The drawing progresses into detailed anatomical drawing, focusing on landmarks, rib cage, pelvis, and muscle placement. Knowledge of anatomy is key to informing the muscle definition in the drawing, accurately conveying the character's physical state and emotional journey.

Light and Shadow

  • While optional, steps to enhance the three-dimensional appearance through lighting and value are discussed. These elements are crucial in character design, adding depth and realism to the drawing.

Symbolism and Narrative

  • Abby's choice of weapon, a hammer, is not arbitrary but symbolizes her psychology and narrative arc. It emphasizes themes of revenge and transformation, which are central to her character design.

* * *

Understanding anatomy, gesture, and lighting is paramount in character design, especially when the goal is storytelling through art. This comprehensive approach to drawing, as demonstrated with Abby's character, encourages a deeper understanding of these elements, enabling artists to convey complex narratives and character evolutions effectively.
