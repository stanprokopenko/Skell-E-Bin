How to Combine Species for Creature Design

Copy Code

Combining species is like creating a monster by merging two different sources. The key to successful creature design is the importance of observation and analysis. By breaking down shapes into bold, objective forms, you can create unique and compelling creatures.

Initial Inspiration and Structure

Start with an ape as your primary inspiration for movement and skeletal structure. Then, combine the ape with another species or element to create something new and exciting.

Starting with Heads

Focus on initial shapes without preconceived notions. Gesture the initial shapes, perceiving beyond details. This helps in capturing the essence of the creature.

Key Features to Consider

  • Muzzle shapes and eye placement: These are crucial for defining the character of your creature.
  • Ears: Consider cone-shaped ears that appear as if you can see down into them.
  • Mouth and chin shapes: These add personality to your creature.
  • Big brow and protruding forehead: Notice these features to add depth.
  • Angle of the nose: Especially important in profile views.

Breaking Down Shapes

Jump around when breaking down shapes to maintain harmony. Analyze simple and objective side planes and wrinkles. Measure proportions and adjust as needed to ensure everything fits together well.

Observing Fur and Mane Features

Observe fur patterns and mane-like features. Approach drawing like a painting, not getting caught up in one area. This helps in maintaining a cohesive look.

Flow and Design

Look for flow in your design, where one line and shape flows into another. Use digital tools to adjust and refine shapes, ensuring a smooth and natural look.

Detailed Studies and Creativity

Enhance your visual vocabulary and creativity through detailed studies. Draw from multiple angles to understand shapes better. This practice will make your designs more robust and versatile.

Combining Elements

Combine elements from bats and chimps to create unique creatures. Focus on asymmetry, using a 70-30 rule rather than perfect balance. Exploit interesting features like the bat's nose and the chimp's mane to add uniqueness.

Cohesive Design

Combine brow ridges and other features to create a cohesive design. Rough out shapes to find appealing combinations. This helps in creating a balanced and interesting creature.

Professional Considerations

In professional settings, consider production designer and director notes. Aim for a balance of realism and character in your creature design. This ensures your designs are both believable and engaging.

Independent Analysis and Feedback

Analyze species independently before combining them. Submit your work to the Proko community for feedback and inspiration. This will help you improve and refine your designs.

Learning from Experts

Learn techniques for stronger character and creature drawings from experts like David Colman. This will enhance your skills and broaden your understanding of creature design.
