Penciling Comics: From Layout to Finished Page

Transferring the Reduced Size Layout

To begin, trace your reduced size layout onto the full size page using a light box or a computer monitor. Focus on loose placement rather than copying precise details. The goal is to establish the general composition and layout of the page.

Tightening Up the Page

Once you've transferred the layout, it's time to tighten up the page. Work through the following steps systematically to address and fix any issues at each stage of the process.

Establishing Panel Borders and Perspective

  • Establish the panel borders for each panel on the page.
  • Lay in proper perspective for each panel, especially for larger scenes. This will help create depth and a sense of space.

Sketching and Tightening Elements

  • Re-establish your panels piece by piece.
  • Lightly sketch each element within the panels before tightening them up.
  • Use proper construction techniques for faces, figures, and environmental elements.
  • Avoid getting too detailed at this point. Focus on drawing the cleanest, simplest version of your full-size page.

Adding Line Weight, Shadows, and Rendering

  • Once you've re-established the whole page as a tightline drawing, move on to adding line weight, shadows, and rendering.
  • Use line weight to create depth, distinguish foreground from background, and emphasize important elements.
  • Add shadows to create volume and depth, and to establish the lighting in each panel.
  • Render the page to add texture, detail, and a sense of realism.

Digital Workflow (Optional)

Feel free to work digitally if that's your preferred method. Digital tools can offer flexibility, the ability to easily make changes, and a streamlined workflow.

Submitting Assignments

To have a chance at being in the critique video, follow these steps:

  • Post images of your final reduced size layout along with your final pencils.
  • Submit your assignments within the next month.

Good luck with your comic book page! Remember to work systematically, address issues as they arise, and have fun with the process.
