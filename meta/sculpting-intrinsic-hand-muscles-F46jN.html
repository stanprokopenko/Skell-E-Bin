Sculpting Intrinsic Hand Muscles

Copy Code

In this lesson, you'll add muscles to a skeleton sculpture of a hand. Use the provided 3D model reference and focus on intrinsic muscles, which are located from the wrist up, mainly in the palm and back of the hand. Remember, muscle bellies do not extend into the digits or phalanges.

Materials and Preparation

  • Types of clay: Use simple strips of clay for adding muscles. If using polymer clay for bones, use non-dry modeling clay for muscles to allow for later removal.
  • Color differentiation: Consider using a different color for muscles, such as reddish clay, to distinguish them from bones.
  • Mixing materials: Mix microcrystalline wax with red wax from Babybel cheese for a suitable muscle material.

General Guidelines

  • Adding muscles: Pay attention to connection points and general shape. Keep muscles flat and removable to avoid damaging the underlying sculpture.
  • Starting point: Begin with deep muscles and work outward.

Step-by-Step Muscle Addition

Opponens Pollicis

  • Add a rectangular strip from the metacarpal of the thumb to a wrist bone.

Adductor Pollicis Transverse

  • Add a triangular muscle from the middle phalanx of the thumb to the middle palm.

Adductor Pollicis Brevis

  • Attach this muscle to the middle phalanx of the thumb.

Flexor Pollicis Brevis Superficialis

  • Add a longer, thinner triangular muscle.

First Lumbrical

  • Attach this muscle to the outside part of the first knuckle of the pointer finger.

First Palmar Interosseous

  • Attach this muscle to the inside part of the first knuckle of the pointer finger.

Third Lumbrical

  • Add a bipennate muscle between the middle and ring fingers.

Third Palmar Interosseous and Fourth Lumbrical

  • Both muscles attach to the inside part of the pinky.

Opponens Digiti Minimi

  • Add this muscle from the wrist bones to the metacarpal of the pinky.

Adductor Digiti Minimi

  • This is a larger muscle of the pinky.

Palmaris Brevis

  • Add a strip-like muscle that helps grip by pinching the skin of the pinky mass.

Dorsal Interosseous Muscles

  • First dorsal interosseous: A teardrop-shaped muscle at the back of the thumb.
  • Fourth dorsal interosseous: Located between the pinky and ring finger.
  • Third and second dorsal interosseous muscles: Add these as well.

Assignment and Practice

Completing assignments is crucial for learning and improvement. Dedicate about 30-40 minutes to add these muscles to your hand sculpture. Submit images of your work for critiques and feedback.

* * *

After following these steps, you should have a detailed hand sculpture with muscles.
