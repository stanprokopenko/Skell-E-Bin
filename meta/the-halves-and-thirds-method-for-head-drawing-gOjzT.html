Welcome to step five, focusing on proportions in head drawing. Previously, you set up the gesture, shaped the big components, and organized the head's relationship to the neck and body. Now, you'll dig into basic proportions using a rough system of halves and thirds.

Understanding Landmarks

Consider landmarks as any bone visible through the skin. Drawing from skulls can help you understand these landmarks, like the Proko-made Loomis skull.

The Brow Ridge

Return to the placement of the brow from previous steps. The brow ridge is not the eyebrow but the projection of bone at the bottom of the forehead.

  • Gender differences: Women typically have a vertical forehead, while men have a diagonal one.
  • The brow ridge protects the eye and can develop a superciliary arch in hyper-masculine men.

Gender Differences in Facial Features

  • The chin can also show gender differences: men might have a broader, boxier chin, while women might have a softer, more recessed chin.

Proportional Breakdown Using Halves and Thirds

Break up proportions using halves and thirds.

  • The first half between the brow and chin is the base of the nose. Focus on where the nose is flush with the face, not the cartilage or nostrils.
  • Think of head construction as dissolving the figure into geometries, aiming for an ideal version.

Key Facial Structures and Their Proportions

  • The nasal aperture is a key shape, varying in form and contributing to likeness.
  • The bottom of the socket is halfway between the brow and the base of the nose.
  • The cheekbone creates the bottom of the socket and connects various parts of the face.
  • The center of the eye and the keystone (glabella) are halfway between the brow and the bottom of the socket. The keystone helps space the eyes correctly.

Dividing the Area from Nose to Chin

Divide the area from the base of the nose to the chin into three equal parts.

  • The first line marks where the lips part.
  • The second line indicates the top of the chin.

These divisions are averages to help organize the big idea, allowing for adjustments.

Gender Indicators in the Mastoid Process and Jaw Angles

The mastoid process and jaw angles can indicate gender differences.

Integrating Proportions into Initial Steps

Focus on simple halves and thirds, but acknowledge the wealth of knowledge for advancing likeness. Integrate these proportions back into your initial four steps.
