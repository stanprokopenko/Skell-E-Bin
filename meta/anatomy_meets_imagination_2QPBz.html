Studying Anatomy in Two Stages

When drawing creatures, it's helpful to study anatomy in two stages:

  1. Stage 1: Bone structure

    • Example: For cats, study the shoulder blade, the bone that juts back, the elbow, and the arm bones down to the wrist and paw.

  2. Stage 2: Form

    • This is the simplistic version of what we see on the outside.
    • Example: A cat's arm is a tube shape that's wider at the top, narrowing down to the wrist, and turning into the paw.
    • Combine the knowledge of the bone with the simple outside form for a comprehensive understanding.

Creating Fantasy Creatures

  • Combining reptile and cat anatomy

    • Use wire form anatomy structures to create the basic shape.
    • Incorporate lizard features, such as a flat body on the ground, with cat features, like a regal, upright posture.

  • Adding unique elements

    • Example: Create a crown of spikes starting at the eye bridge and wrapping around the head of a lizard creature.

  • Drawing inspiration from personal preferences

    • Pull elements that stand out to you from various creatures, such as frail, spindly legs and big eyes for dragons.

Developing a Personal Art Style

  • Accumulating style over time

    • Pull bits and pieces from the real world and artists you admire.
    • Your style will develop naturally as you combine these elements.

  • Focusing on technical skills

    • Don't feel pressured to have a style right away.
    • Let your style develop in the background as you focus on learning technical skills.

Experimenting with Art Materials and Techniques

  • Mixing up materials

    • Never get too comfortable with your current materials.
    • Try new materials to discover new drawing preferences and techniques.
    • Example: Learning charcoal at Watts Atelier to fill in gaps in traditional art skills.

  • Using overlaps and folds

    • Create overlaps, such as the tail overlapping the foot, to bring dimension and realism to sketches.
    • Pay attention to folds and creases where joints meet to show how the form changes.

The Importance of Storytelling in Art

  • Breaking down the storytelling process

    • Storytelling is a learnable skill set, not an innate trait.
    • Practice and learn how to come up with creative ideas and stories in your art.
