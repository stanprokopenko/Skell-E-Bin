Hi, my name is Michael Hampton. Welcome to the head drawing and construction course.

In this course, you're going to learn a step-by-step approach that will guide you through understanding the proportions of the face, developing primary structures for your skull and jaw, and addressing the features as sculptures set on top of those primary forms.

This is an extremely perspective-heavy and form-heavy investigation into the development of the head and face. This system is designed to aid you in the invention of heads. You can take this approach as something strict to get a consistent result, but you can also plug in your own interests, concerns, and ways of thinking about the head and skull. It can be easily compatible with your personal style.

Following each lesson, there will be assignments that I will explain and give to you. By the end of this course, I want you to have a consistent workflow that allows you to set up a head from beginning to end.

This course will help with many things: - Invention: Building a backbone and scaffolding for the way you think about light and shadow. - Understanding plane and surface: Informing your use of value and edge. - Character creation: You can distort and create various heads or characters by pushing and pulling these shapes.

Check it out at proko.com/hampton. I'll see you there.
