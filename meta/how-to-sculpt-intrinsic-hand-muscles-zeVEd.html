How to Sculpt Intrinsic Hand Muscles

Copy Code

Now that you've sculpted the skeleton of the hand, it's time to add the muscles. In this lesson, you'll focus on the intrinsic muscles from the wrist up, located in the palm and on the front and back of the hand. Remember, the muscle bellies don't extend into the digits or phalanges.

Materials and Techniques

To add muscles to your hand sculpture, you'll use simple strips of clay pressed into rough shapes. You have several options for materials:

  • Use different colors for muscles (e.g., reddish) to distinguish them from bones
  • Consider using polymer clay for bones and non-drying clay for muscles
  • The instructor used microcrystalline wax mixed with red wax from Babybel cheese

Sculpting Techniques

When adding muscles:

  • Press simple strips of clay into rough shapes
  • Keep muscles relatively flat for this exercise
  • Consider adding muscles in a removable way to preserve the bone structure
  • Begin with deep muscles and work outward
  • Pay attention to muscle connection points (point A and B) and general shape

Adding Palmar Muscles

Start with the muscles on the palm side of the hand:

  1. Opponens pollicis: Create a rectangular strip from the thumb metacarpal to the wrist bone.

  2. Adductor pollicis transverse: Form a triangular shape connecting the thumb's middle phalanx to the palm.

  3. Adductor pollicis brevis: Attach this to the thumb's middle phalanx.

  4. Flexor pollicis brevis superficialis: Add a longer, thinner triangular muscle next to the adductor pollicis brevis.

  5. First lumbrical: Attach this to the outside of the pointer finger's first knuckle.

  6. First palmar interosseous: Connect the inside of the pointer finger's first knuckle to the metacarpal.

  7. Third lumbrical: Create a bipennate muscle between the middle and ring fingers.

  8. Third palmar interosseus and fourth lumbrical: Attach these to the inside of the pinky's first knuckle.

  9. Opponens digiti minimi: This muscle travels from the wrist bones to the pinky metacarpal.

  10. Adductor digiti minimi: Add this larger muscle in the hypothenar eminence.

  11. Palmaris brevis: Create a strip-like muscle wrapping over the hypothenar muscles.

Adding Dorsal Muscles

Now, move to the back of the hand:

  1. First dorsal interosseous: Form a teardrop shape at the back of the thumb. This is an important muscle mass.

  2. Fourth, third, and second dorsal interosseous muscles: Add these between the fingers on the back of the hand.

* * *

Your assignment is to add these muscles to your hand skeleton sculpture. This process should take you about 30-60 minutes.

Remember, the key to improving your skills is practice. Complete this assignment and consider submitting images for critique. The more you practice, the better you'll become at understanding and recreating the complex anatomy of the hand.
