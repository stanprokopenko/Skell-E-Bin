Google's AI Integrations and Updates

Google's recent IO event showcased their ambitious plans to integrate generative AI into a wide array of services. Here are the key highlights:

  • Public Rollout of Gemini-Infused Google Search Results: Google is starting to roll out search results enhanced with Gemini, providing AI-curated results and AI overview summaries for open-ended queries. A dedicated web search tab will filter out Google's widgets and doodads, focusing on text-based results.

  • Updates to AI Models:

    • Gemini 1.5 Pro: Now available to all developers, featuring a 2 million token context window.
    • Gemini Nano: Enhanced with multimodal capabilities for images, audio, and video.
    • Gemini 1.5 Flash: A faster model with lower latency.

  • Integration in Google's Workplace Suite: Gemini is now integrated as a panel in Google's workplace suite, which can be toggled on or off. An AI teammate feature is also introduced to track tasks and coordinate communications with co-workers.

  • New Models Announced:

    • Imagine 3: A new text-to-image model.
    • Google DeepMind's Vo: A generative video model, available to a few creators through the new Video FX tool and integrated with Google's AI watermarking system, SynthID.

  • Project Astra: Google's vision for future AI assistants that can perceive live video and audio and respond in real-time.

  • DeepMind's AlphaFold 3: A new tool for protein structure prediction, hyped by Google CEO Sundar Pichai.

AI Accessibility Features

Google announced several new AI accessibility features for Android:

  • Monitoring Phone Calls for Potential Scams: AI will help monitor and identify potential scam calls.
  • Describing Images for Visually Impaired Users: AI will provide descriptions of images to assist visually impaired users.
  • Controlling a Cursor with Head and Face Movements: Users can control a cursor using head and face movements, enhancing accessibility.

Personnel Changes in OpenAI

OpenAI has seen significant personnel changes recently:

  • Ilia Sutskever: The co-founder and chief scientist of OpenAI has left the company for unknown reasons.
  • Jan Leike: The co-leader of OpenAI's Superalignment group has also resigned.
  • High-Profile Departures: These resignations are part of a series of high-profile departures from OpenAI.

Public Opinion and Government Actions on AI

Public opinion and government actions on AI are evolving:

  • YouGov Survey: Found that 63% of Americans support government legislation to prevent superintelligent AI.
  • US Senate's Roadmap: The Senate revealed a $32 billion annual roadmap for regulating AI, focusing primarily on non-military AI innovation.

Other Tech Industry Updates

Several other notable updates in the tech industry include:

  • Apple's iOS 17.5 Update: Causing old, deleted photos to reappear in users' photo albums.
  • Leaked Dell Documents: Show a bullish attitude towards Qualcomm's upcoming Snapdragon X Elite chips.
  • Federal Investigation of Waymo: Alphabet subsidiary Waymo is under investigation due to self-driving car crashes.
  • FTC's Notice to Automakers: The FTC has warned automakers to stop selling connected cars that illegally collect user data.
  • New York City's AI-Enabled Gun Scanners: NYC will test AI-enabled gun scanners in its subway system, despite previous tests showing high false positives.
  • TikTok's Testing of ChatGPT-Generated Answers: TikTok is testing ChatGPT-generated answers in its search results, with warnings about potential inaccuracies. Previously, TikTok tested supplementing search results with links to Google, Wikipedia, IMDb, and other websites.
