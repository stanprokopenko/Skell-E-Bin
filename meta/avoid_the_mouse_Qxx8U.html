Avoid the Mouse

Copy Code

When diving into digital painting, it's crucial to use pressure-sensitive devices. A mouse just won't cut it because it lacks pressure sensitivity, leading to artwork that misses out on subtlety. That's why I insist on students using a pressure-sensitive tablet. This tool is non-negotiable for achieving nuanced artwork.

Recommended Hardware

Pressure-Sensitive Tablets

The importance of a pressure-sensitive tablet cannot be overstated for creating nuanced artwork. My go-to recommendation is Wacom tablets. They're the industry standard, and while they come with a higher price tag, their quality and reliability justify the cost.

Alternative Tablets

For those looking for more budget-friendly options, tablets from XP-Pen or Huion serve as great alternatives. They offer a good balance between cost and functionality.

iPads for Digital Art

iPads stand out for digital art due to their portability, direct screen feedback, and compatibility with Procreate. Procreate is an affordable app that doesn't require a subscription, making it an excellent choice for artists on the go.

Screen Tablets

For professional artists, drawing directly on screens is ideal. Devices like the Wacom Cintiq, as well as similar products from Huion and XP-Pen, eliminate the hand-eye coordination challenge. Though they come at a higher cost, the investment is worth it for the level of professionalism they bring to your work.

Software Recommendations

Industry Standard Software

Adobe Photoshop is the gold standard for concept design and illustration. However, its subscription model can be a barrier due to the cost.

Affordable and Free Alternatives

For those on a tight budget, Krita offers a viable solution as a free software. Other affordable tools like Affinity Photo and Clip Studio Paint are also worth considering. These options ensure that budget constraints don't hinder your creative process.

Teaching Approach

My teaching will be software agnostic, meaning students are free to choose the tool that best fits their budget and preference. The emphasis is on the fact that the art itself is more important than the specific software used. This approach ensures that all students, regardless of their financial situation, have the opportunity to learn and grow as digital artists.
