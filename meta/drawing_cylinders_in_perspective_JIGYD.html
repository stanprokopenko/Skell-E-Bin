Understanding Ovals and Ellipses

First off, let's clear up some confusion between ovals and ellipses. While all ellipses are ovals, not all ovals are ellipses. An ellipse has a specific mathematical definition and must have four equal quadrants. To check if an oval is an ellipse, you can perform the fold test: fold it along the longest part and then again perpendicular to it. If the four quadrants align perfectly, it's an ellipse. The longest line is called the major axis, and the short line perpendicular to it is the minor axis.

Ellipses in Perspective Drawing

When we talk about ellipses in perspective drawing, we're essentially referring to circles tilted in 3D space. As a circle tilts more, it becomes foreshortened, eventually appearing as a line. A circle viewed straight on is a perfect circle, but when tilted, it becomes an ellipse. The major axis of an ellipse in perspective doesn't cross the center but remains symmetrical two-dimensionally. This is because the closer half of the ellipse appears bigger than the farther half.

Drawing Cylinders with Ellipses

A cylinder can be thought of as a stack of ellipses. When drawing a cylinder, it's crucial to at least have the two ends represented by ellipses. The angle of the minor axis indicates the orientation of these ellipses. For example, the minor axis of a barbell's plate aligns with the bar, serving as the z-axis leading to the vanishing point. This principle also applies to car wheels, where the minor and major axes help determine the ellipse's orientation.

Drawing a Cylinder in a Box

Sometimes, it's helpful to draw a box in perspective first and then fit a cylinder within it. This approach aids in understanding the cylinder's perspective. Here's how to do it:

  • Start with a square and tilt it to create a foreshortened square in perspective.
  • Draw an X across the square to find the midpoint, which helps locate the four points where the circle (and thus the ellipse) will touch the square's edges.
  • The minor axis of the ellipse should align with the square's angle, crossing the center point.
  • Sketch the ellipse lightly, ensuring it touches the identified points and aligns with the minor and major axes.

This method helps visualize how the ellipse needs to widen and narrow to fit within the foreshortened square.

Practical Tips for Sketching Cylinders

When sketching cylinders without drawing a box every time, remember these key points:

  • The angle of the ellipse is perpendicular to the length of the cylinder.
  • The farther end of the cylinder will have a wider ellipse.
  • The sides of the cylinder will converge slightly as they move away from the viewer.

Practice these concepts to get a feel for drawing cylinders in perspective accurately.
