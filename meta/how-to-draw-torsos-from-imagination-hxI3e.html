How to Draw Torsos from Imagination

Copy Code

When drawing torsos, focus on the shoulders, rib cage, and hips. Visualize the torso as a wide, tall box that you can turn, twist, and lean. This approach helps simplify the complex structure of the torso and makes it easier to understand and draw.

Level One: Drawing from Imagination

  1. Draw Multiple Pages of Torsos: Start by drawing several pages of torsos from your imagination.
  2. Start with the Shoulder Plane: Begin each drawing with the shoulder plane, similar to gestural perspective.
  3. Visualize Hip Movement: Imagine how the hips will move as the box comes down.
  4. Use Cross-Contour Lines: Divide the box into sections with cross-contour lines to help visualize deformation.
  5. Adjust Box Proportions: Modify the box's proportions based on the body type (e.g., husky build vs. tall skinny person).
  6. Experiment with Positions and Deformations: Try different positions and deformations of the box.
  7. Practice Twisting the Box: Twist the box to see different planes and angles.
  8. Make Intuitive Adjustments: Adjust the box intuitively without relying on vanishing points.
  9. Fill Several Pages: Draw at least 20 boxes to get comfortable with the process.
  10. Seek Critique: Post confusing drawings for critique and try different approaches to resolve issues.

Level Two: Drawing from Photos

  1. Start with the Shoulder Plane: Begin by drawing the shoulder plane from the photo.
  2. Imagine the Box: Visualize the torso as a box.
  3. Pay Attention to Angles and Curves: Focus on the angles and curves in the photos, avoiding distractions from clothing.
  4. Use Gestural Rhythm Lines: Sketch gestural rhythm lines to find the action in the pose.
  5. Decide on Visible Planes and Center Lines: Determine the visible planes and center lines based on the photo's perspective.
  6. Bloat or Taper the Box: Add realism by bloating or tapering the box, especially around the rib cage.
  7. Exaggerate Poses: Push the differences from a neutral pose to exaggerate the poses.
  8. Compare to a Baseline: Compare the pose to a baseline and push the differences further.
  9. Avoid Over-Exaggeration: Be careful not to over-exaggerate to prevent breaking the form.
  10. Exaggerate Relationships: Focus on exaggerating the relationships between different parts of the box.
  11. Interpret Exaggerations: Practice interpreting exaggerations in multiple ways, focusing on twisting and crunching.

Advanced Practice: Exaggeration (Level 2.5)

For further improvement, practice exaggeration as a more advanced level. Focus on twisting and crunching the box to see how far you can push the forms while maintaining a believable structure.

Sharing and Feedback

Complete the assignments and share your progress for feedback. This will help you identify areas for improvement and refine your technique.
