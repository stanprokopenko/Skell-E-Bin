How to Refine Head Drawings

Drawing heads and necks can be challenging, but with the right techniques and awareness of common pitfalls, artists can significantly improve their work. This article covers essential tips and techniques for creating accurate and dynamic head and neck drawings.

Common Difficulties in Head Drawing

Many students struggle with specific areas when drawing heads. The area under the jaw and finding the correct brow line are particularly challenging. These difficulties often stem from issues with proportions and perspective.

Basic Techniques and Concepts

Head Structure

When drawing the head, it's important to understand its basic structure. The jaw length follows a one-third to two-thirds ratio, but only when the head is facing straight forward. This ratio changes when the head is tilted back.

A helpful technique is to draw an elliptical line through the ball of the head to connect the brow to the ear. This line serves as a crucial perspective determination for the rest of the head. To find the bottom of the jaw, try replicating this ellipse under the chin.

Neck Drawing

When drawing necks, aim for asymmetry in the lines to avoid too much parallelism. Always connect the neck to the head with a T-shape, creating an overlap or wrapping line. It's important to subtly indicate stretch and pinch in the neck.

Make necks narrower than they appear in real life to allow room for adding anatomy later. For back views, relate the curve of the brow to the base of the skull.

Proportions and Perspective

The brow line serves as a perspective determination for the rest of the head. It's crucial to match tilts and perspectives across the drawing for consistency. Use centerlines and underplanes to establish structure and maintain proper proportions.

Advanced Techniques

Form Intersections and Connections

Use wrapping lines where forms intersect, such as where the under plane of the jaw meets the neck. Work on refining the intersection of forms using T-shapes and wrapping lines. Focus on tying forms together through intersections, using wrapping, overlapping, and opposing angles to join forms.

Specific Views and Angles

For profiles, avoid straight lines and exaggerate direction slightly under the eyebrow and jaw. In extreme views, the jaw can move up or down relative to the "egg" shape of the head.

For front views, push the neck out to avoid symmetry and stiffness. In back views, show the neck going away from the viewer.

Refining Your Drawings

Consider customizing jaw shapes to appear more feminine or masculine. Focus on how the canopy of the jaw relates to other facial features. Ensure the tilt of the face matches the overall tilt established for the head.

Common Mistakes and How to Avoid Them

Proportion Issues

Be careful with the distance between the jaw and the rest of the head; it can appear too long. Avoid making jaws too short or heads too big. Keep neck size average early on for easier refinement later.

Perspective and Alignment Problems

Ensure that angles of different facial planes are consistent with each other. Keep the front plane of the face more parallel to avoid warping features later. Make sure the brow to ear lines align properly.

Form and Structure Errors

Avoid drawing necks too deep. Keep face curves moderate, avoiding excessive bending. Be cautious of becoming too contour-oriented early on. Avoid outlines that make shapes feel disconnected.

Tips for Improvement

  • Practice drawing basic shapes like circles to improve overall drawing skills.
  • Use perspective lines to solve jaw placement issues.
  • Double-check your work using established steps.
  • Focus on clean-up and refinement of initial sketches.
  • Pay attention to form intersections and overlaps.
  • Use the halfway line of the sphere to ensure proper placement of features.

Remember, the early stages of construction are crucial for the overall quality of the final product. Most issues in later stages can be traced back to problems in the initial phases. By focusing on these techniques and being aware of common pitfalls, you can significantly improve your head and neck drawings.
