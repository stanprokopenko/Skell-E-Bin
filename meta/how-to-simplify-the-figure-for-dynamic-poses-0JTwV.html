How to Simplify the Figure for Dynamic Poses

Copy Code

Understanding how to simplify the figure is key to drawing believable poses. Start with a gesture drawing and construct simplified forms on top.

Gesture Drawing Basics

Begin with a simple sphere and develop the gesture based on 16 to 17 lines. Gesture simplifies the directional aim of a part, and the spacing between lines represents joints.

Considering Different Views

Consider different views: front, front three-quarter, and more challenging angles.

Observing Weight Distribution

Observe weight distribution and use wrapping lines to indicate volume. Use long relational rhythms in passive parts of the body for a natural look.

Drawing Arms and Elbows

For arms crossing the body, note the elbow's position and the overall directional balance.

Reference Points for Balance

Use the pit of the neck as a reference point for balance; a stable pose aligns this point with the weight-bearing ankle. Push poses to avoid overly balanced stances.

Constructing Shapes

Construct shapes: pelvis, rib cage, and connections. Use symmetry line landmarks: pit of the neck, belly button, pelvis.

Indicating Rotation and Twists

A C-curve in the symmetry line indicates a directional rotation or dimensional turn. For twists, use an S-curve: rib cage and pelvis face opposite directions. Manage twists with asymmetrical triangles representing muscle attachments.

Constructing Rib Cage and Pelvis

Construct boxes for rib cage and pelvis, ensuring parallel lines for width, depth, and height.

Drawing Limbs

Avoid using tapered cylinders for limbs early on; focus on bone direction and perspective.

Drawing Legs

For legs, use rhythm lines to establish width and form, then add muscle and fat.

Studying Feet

Study feet by constructing basic shapes and foreshortening them. Use a deck of cards analogy for the foot's structure, considering its arches.

Drawing Arms

For arms, create perspectives and use cylinders for upper and lower arms. Draw through forms to see all sides and complete ellipses.

Drawing Hands

For hands, use a two-by-four analogy to understand planes and connections.

Maintaining Lyrical Feeling

Maintain a lyrical feeling in construction to avoid stiffness. Over-exaggerate gestures to anticipate stiffening during construction.

Combining Gesture and Anatomy

Combine gesture and anatomy for a comprehensive approach to figure drawing.
